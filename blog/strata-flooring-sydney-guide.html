Strata Flooring in Sydney: A Guide for Body Corporates and Strata Managers

Published by Premrest | Strata Flooring Specialists

Sydney has the largest strata market in Australia. With over 80,000 strata schemes across the greater metro area, there are a lot of lobbies, corridors, stairwells and common areas that need flooring — and a lot of that flooring is due for replacement.

Whether you're managing a harbourside apartment block in the Eastern Suburbs, a high-rise in Parramatta, or a boutique building in Surry Hills, this guide covers everything you need to know about strata flooring in Sydney.

Sydney's Strata Market: Why Flooring Matters

Sydney's strata buildings range from sprawling 1970s complexes to brand-new towers, and they all share one thing: common area flooring that needs to handle heavy daily use. With Sydney's property values being what they are, the condition of your common areas directly affects property values and resident satisfaction.

Common flooring challenges specific to Sydney strata buildings include:

  • Humidity and coastal salt air accelerating wear on unprotected flooring
  • High foot traffic in dense inner-city buildings
  • Water tracking from Sydney's frequent rain events
  • Ageing flooring in the large number of buildings from the 1990s and 2000s apartment boom
  • Strict strata by-laws governing renovation and noise requirements

Best Flooring Options for Sydney Strata Buildings

Carpet Tiles for Lobbies and Corridors

Carpet tiles remain the most popular choice for Sydney strata corridors and lobbies. They're durable, available in hundreds of colours and patterns, and the ability to replace individual tiles makes them incredibly practical for strata buildings where damage from moving furniture, deliveries and daily life is inevitable.

For Sydney buildings, acoustic performance is particularly important. NSW strata legislation includes provisions around noise transfer, and carpet tiles provide significantly better sound absorption than hard flooring. Modular Carpet offers commercial-grade ranges with enhanced acoustic ratings specifically suited to strata environments.

Best for: Corridors, lobbies, lift landings, common lounges, meeting rooms

Vinyl Planks for Wet and High-Traffic Areas

Vinyl plank flooring (LVT) is essential for Sydney strata buildings, particularly in areas exposed to moisture. Sydney's climate means building entries regularly deal with wet conditions, and vinyl planks provide the waterproof durability these areas need.

Modern vinyl planks are virtually indistinguishable from real timber or stone at first glance, making them a popular upgrade for Sydney strata buildings wanting a contemporary look. For a detailed breakdown, see our carpet tiles vs vinyl planks comparison.

Best for: Entry foyers, ground-floor lobbies, kitchens, laundries, pool areas

Hybrid and Specialty Flooring

Hybrid flooring offers a rigid core with a vinyl surface — combining the stability of laminate with the water resistance of vinyl. It's an excellent choice for Sydney strata buildings that want premium timber aesthetics in areas where real timber would be impractical.

For buildings with gyms, rubber flooring provides the durability and impact absorption needed. For meeting rooms, libraries or acoustic-sensitive spaces, cork flooring offers natural sound insulation and a distinctive warm feel underfoot.

NSW Strata Legislation and Flooring Approvals

In New South Wales, the Strata Schemes Management Act 2015 governs how common property is managed. Flooring replacement in common areas typically requires:

  1. Obtaining quotes — The strata committee should obtain competitive quotes that include product specifications, timelines and full costs
  2. Committee or general meeting approval — Depending on the value and the scheme's by-laws, approval may be at committee level or may require an ordinary resolution at a general meeting
  3. 10-year capital works plan — Flooring replacement should ideally be included in the scheme's capital works fund plan, making budgeting more predictable
  4. By-law compliance — Some schemes have specific by-laws about flooring types, acoustic ratings and installation hours

Cost Considerations for Sydney Strata Flooring

Sydney strata flooring costs are influenced by:

  • Building height and access — High-rise buildings require materials to be transported via service lifts, adding to labour time
  • Working hour restrictions — Many Sydney strata schemes limit construction work to specific hours (e.g., 8am–5pm weekdays)
  • Subfloor preparation — Older Sydney buildings may need moisture barriers, levelling compounds or old adhesive removal
  • Product selection — Premium vinyl planks and designer carpet tiles cost more but last longer and maintain appearance better
  • Staging requirements — Work often needs to be staged to keep corridors accessible, which can extend timelines

Maintaining Strata Floors in Sydney's Climate

Sydney's humid subtropical climate means flooring maintenance is not optional — it's essential. Without a proper floor maintenance program, carpet tiles can develop mould in humid conditions, and vinyl can lose its protective coating faster than in drier climates.

Our Sydney strata maintenance programs include:

  • Six-monthly professional deep cleans using low moisture methods
  • Protective sealant applications for vinyl and hard flooring
  • Spot cleaning and individual tile replacement
  • Mould prevention treatments for buildings in humid or coastal locations
  • Annual condition reports for the owners corporation
