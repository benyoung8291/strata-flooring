The Complete Guide to Strata Flooring in Melbourne

Published by Premrest | Strata Flooring Specialists

Melbourne is home to thousands of strata buildings — from the towering apartment complexes of Southbank and Docklands to the heritage-listed walk-ups of Carlton and Fitzroy. Every one of them has common areas with flooring that takes a daily beating from foot traffic, trolleys, pets and weather.

If you're a strata manager or body corporate committee member in Melbourne, flooring replacement is likely on your radar. This guide covers everything you need to know about choosing, installing and maintaining flooring in Melbourne strata buildings.

Why Melbourne Strata Buildings Need Specialist Flooring

Strata common areas are fundamentally different from residential or even standard commercial spaces. Lobbies, corridors, stairwells and shared amenities see constant use from dozens — sometimes hundreds — of residents every day. The flooring needs to handle that volume while still looking sharp.

Melbourne's climate adds another layer of complexity. The city's famously unpredictable weather means lobbies regularly deal with wet shoes, dripping umbrellas and tracked-in mud. Winter months bring sustained rain, while summer can see everything from scorching heat to sudden downpours. Your flooring choices need to account for all of it.

Common flooring problems in Melbourne strata buildings include:

  • Stained and worn carpet in lobbies and corridors
  • Peeling or lifting vinyl in high-traffic zones
  • Water damage near building entrances
  • Faded or outdated flooring that drags down the building's appearance
  • Noise complaints from hard flooring in corridors

Best Flooring Options for Melbourne Strata Buildings

Carpet Tiles

Carpet tiles are the most popular choice for Melbourne strata lobbies and corridors. They're durable, come in a massive range of colours and patterns, and — crucially — can be replaced individually when damaged. If a resident spills coffee in the lobby or a delivery trolley scuffs a section, you replace those tiles rather than re-carpeting the entire area.

For Melbourne buildings, carpet tiles also offer excellent acoustic benefits. In multi-storey buildings where corridor noise is a common complaint, carpet tiles absorb sound far better than hard flooring. Specialists like Modular Carpet offer ranges specifically designed for high-traffic commercial and strata environments.

Best for: Lobbies, corridors, lift landings, common lounges

Vinyl Planks

Vinyl plank flooring (also called luxury vinyl tile or LVT) is the go-to for areas where moisture is a concern. In Melbourne strata buildings, this typically means entry foyers, kitchens in common rooms, laundries and any area near external doors where rain gets tracked in.

Modern vinyl planks come in realistic timber and stone looks that suit everything from contemporary Docklands apartments to traditional South Yarra buildings. They're waterproof, scratch-resistant and easy to clean — perfect for high-traffic strata environments. For a detailed comparison, see our guide on carpet tiles vs vinyl planks for strata.

Best for: Entry foyers, kitchens, laundries, wet areas, ground-floor corridors

Hybrid Flooring

Hybrid flooring combines the waterproof properties of vinyl with a rigid core for added stability. It's becoming increasingly popular in Melbourne strata buildings that want a premium timber look without the maintenance headaches of real wood.

Best for: Common rooms, lobbies wanting a premium look, mixed-use areas

Cork and Rubber Flooring

For specialised areas within strata buildings, cork flooring offers excellent acoustic properties and natural warmth — ideal for music rooms, libraries or meeting spaces. Rubber flooring is the standard choice for strata gyms, car park stairwells and utility areas where durability and slip resistance are paramount.

Best for: Gyms (rubber), meeting rooms (cork), utility areas (rubber)

Body Corporate Flooring Approval Process in Victoria

In Victoria, flooring replacement in strata common areas typically requires approval from the owners corporation (body corporate). The process usually involves:

  1. Getting quotes — Most committees want at least two or three quotes for comparison
  2. Committee review — The committee reviews options, samples and pricing
  3. Approval at meeting — Depending on the value, this may need a general meeting or can be approved by the committee
  4. Scheduling — Working around building access, resident schedules and any building rules about construction hours

We provide detailed proposals with product specifications, samples, timeline estimates and transparent pricing that make it straightforward for committees and strata managers to present to owners. Cost Considerations for Melbourne Strata Flooring

The cost of strata flooring replacement in Melbourne depends on several factors:

  • Flooring type — Carpet tiles and vinyl planks are the most cost-effective options for common areas
  • Area size — Larger areas benefit from economies of scale
  • Building access — High-rise buildings may need materials moved up via lifts, adding to labour time
  • Subfloor condition — Old adhesive removal, levelling or moisture treatment can add to the scope
  • Working hours — Some Melbourne buildings restrict work to specific hours, which can extend timelines

We provide transparent, no-surprise quotes after a site visit. No hidden costs, no vague line items — just a clear breakdown of what's included.

Floor Maintenance for Melbourne Strata Buildings

Installing new floors is only half the equation. Melbourne's wet winters and dusty summers mean strata flooring needs regular professional care to stay in top condition. A proper floor maintenance program extends the life of your flooring from around 5 years to 15 years or more.

Our maintenance programs for Melbourne strata buildings typically include:

  • Six-monthly low moisture deep cleans
  • Protective sealant treatments for vinyl and hard flooring
  • Spot treatment and individual tile replacement as needed
  • Annual condition reports for the owners corporation
